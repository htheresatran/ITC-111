The Effect on the Economy

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the unemployment rate in March of 2020 increased by 0.9 percentage point to 4.4 percent. This is the highest over-the-month increase rate since January 1975. The number of unemployed people increased from 1.4 million to 7.1 million in March (bls.gov, 2020). The surge in these measures reflects the effects of COVID-19 and the efforts to contain it. Travel, leisure & hospitality, airlines, and other industries are currently experiencing layoffs and may continue to do so, according to Thomas Simons, a senior money market economist at Jefferies LLC (Buchwald, E. 2020).
